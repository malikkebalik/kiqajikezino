    K-pop

    Korean popular music, or K-pop (Korean: RR: keipap), is a genre of popular music that has its roots in South Korea and is a component of that country’s culture. In addition to its origins in traditional Korean music, it incorporates forms and genres from around the world, including pop, hip-hop, R&B, rock, jazz, gospel, reggae, electronic dance, folk, country, disco, and classical.

    In the 2000s, “K-pop” gained popularity, particularly on a global scale. Gayo (Korean: Hanja), which is still frequently used in South Korea, is the word for domestic pop music in Korea.

    Although the term “K-pop” can be used to describe any popular or pop music from South Korea, it is frequently used informally to refer to all Korean music and artists who are connected to the nation’s entertainment and idol industry, regardless of genre.
