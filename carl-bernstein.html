     Journalism Career of Carl Bernstein

    Carl Bernstein, 76, has worked as a journalist his entire life, primarily for “The Washington Post.” He was in charge of several intriguing projects during his time there, including the Watergate incident in June 1972. He and Bob Woodward covered the burglary at the Watergate office building, where five burglars were apprehended by police. As they uncovered information that prompted additional inquiries and President Nixon’s resignation, he gained international notoriety. The two journalists covered their experience on the case in the book “All the President’s Men,” which was published in the wake of the scandal.

    After leaving The Washington Post in 1977, Bernstein participated in a covert CIA-media probe. Before working with ABC news, he later published an article in Rolling Stone. In the midst of all of that, he routinely contributes as a political analyst to various news shows including Newsweek, The Daily Beast, and CNN.

    The Dramatic Married Life of Carl Bernstein

    Carl Bernstein wasn’t as fortunate in his marriages as he was in his professional life. He has split up with his ex-wives Carol Honsa and Nora Ephron twice. In 1968, he wed Carol Honsa, a Washington Post coworker and fellow reporter. Unfortunately, they divorced quite early in 1972 since their marriage could not be maintained.

    In the middle of the 1970s, Bernstein also started dating the writer-director Nora Ephron. Since they got married in 1976, they moved along fairly quickly. They had two boys together, Jacob Bernstein and Max Bernstein, while they were still dating. Unexpectedly, Carl continued to date Margaret Jay, the daughter of the British Prime Minister James Callaghan at the time. There was no turning back because his wife was aware that he was cheating. As a result, the two got divorced in 1980.

    After his second divorce, he dated a number of well-known people, including Bianca Jagger, Martha Stewart, and Elizabeth Taylor. Similar to this, he discovered Christine Kuehbeck, a former model, as a new girlfriend in the early 2000s. In 2003, hoping for luck a third time, the couple exchanged vows.

    Early Years of Carl Bernstein

    Carl Bernstein was born on February 14, 1944, into a secular Jewish household. He was raised in Washington, D.C., where he was born, by his parents, Alfred and Sylvia Bernstein. He attended Montgomery Blair High School in Silver Spring, Maryland, for his education. Similar to that, he continued on to the University of Maryland but left right away to pursue a career in journalism.

    As a copy boy for The Washington Star at the age of 16, Bernstein started his journalism career. Fortunately, he swiftly rose through the ranks to become a reporter through hard manual labor.
