    Carli Miles is a woman who has experienced both the joy and the sorrow of love. She is a travel advisor who helps people plan their dream vacations, but she also knows what it means to lose someone you cherish. She was married to Tyler Skaggs, a talented baseball pitcher who played for the Los Angeles Angels, but he tragically passed away in 2019. Carli Miles is a survivor who honors her husband’s memory by working with his foundation and living with courage and grace.

    Wiki/Bio Facts

    Early Life and Education

    Carli Miles was born on January 26, 1987, in Los Angeles, California. She is the daughter of Nina and Alan Miles, who are both travel advisors and run their own company, Miles2Travel. Carli has a younger brother, Skyler Miles, who is a musician and the founder of King of The Kitchen Music LLC.

    Carli attended Santa Monica High School, where she met her future husband, Tyler Skaggs, who was a year junior to her. They started dating in high school and maintained a long-distance relationship when Tyler pursued his professional baseball career.

    Career as a Travel Advisor

    Carli Miles has been passionate about travel and adventure since she was young and started her career in the travel industry following her education. Carli Miles is a travel advisor who works with her mother, Nina Miles, at Miles 2 Travel, a mother-daughter travel team. They specialize in creating unique and luxury travel experiences for their clients, drawing upon their own personal travel adventures and industry connections. They offer expertly crafted itineraries, exclusive travel perks, and VIP treatment to their customers who want to explore far-reaching corners of the world.

    Carly has visited many countries experiencing different cultures, cuisines, and landscapes. Some of her favorite destinations include Bora Bora, where she got engaged to her late husband Tyler Skaggs, a former MLB pitcher who passed away in 2019, New Zealand, where she went bungee jumping and skydiving, and South Africa, where she witnessed the Big Five on a safari.

    Carli Miles is not only a travel advisor, but also a travel influencer. She has a blog and an Instagram account where she shares her travel tips, stories, and photos with her followers. She also collaborates with other travel bloggers and brands to promote travel-related products and services. She hopes to inspire others to follow their dreams and discover the world.

    Relationship with Tyler Skaggs

    Carli Miles and Tyler Skaggs were high school sweethearts who shared a passion for baseball and travel. They met at Santa Monica High School, where Carli was a senior and Tyler was a junior. Tyler was already a star pitcher who had been drafted by the Los Angeles Angels in 2009.

    They continued their relationship despite the challenges of Tyler’s professional career. Tyler made his MLB debut in 2012 with the Arizona Diamondbacks, and was traded back to the Angels in 2013. He spent six seasons with the Angels, becoming one of their best starters and a beloved teammate.

    Carli supported Tyler throughout his journey, traveling with him to different cities and cheering him on from the stands. She also pursued her own career as a luxury travel advisor, working with her mother at Miles2Travel.

    Carli and Tyler got engaged in 2017, after Tyler proposed to her on a romantic trip to Bora Bora.     Carli was devastated by the sudden death of her husband, whom she had been married to for less than a year. She spoke at his funeral in Santa Monica on July 22, 2019, where she paid tribute to him as her soulmate, lover, confidant, and best friend. She said that he showed her what real love is and that she will love him until her last breath. She also thanked everyone who had supported her during the difficult time and asked them to honor Tyler’s memory by living life to the fullest.

    Aftermath

    Since his death, Carli has been living a relatively quiet life, away from the media spotlight. She has focused on her work as a travel agent and on running the Tyler Skaggs Foundation with Tyler’s mother, Debbie. The foundation aims to empower young people to positively shape their lives through sports, education, and arts. Carli has also been involved in legal actions against the Angels and the employee who allegedly provided Tyler with the fatal drugs. She has said that she wants justice for Tyler and accountability for those who contributed to his death. Later, Eric Prescott Kay, the former communications director of the Angels, was found guilty of providing the drugs that caused Skaggs’s death and sentenced to 22 years in prison. Kay also made insulting comments about Skaggs and his family after his conviction.
